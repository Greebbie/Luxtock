# Example thesis: EV penetration doubling → power semiconductor & charging network bottlenecks

> This is an **illustrative example thesis**, used to demonstrate the structure
> and discipline requirements of a thesis file. The numbers are placeholders,
> not a real judgment. Replace all of the content when writing your own thesis.

Assume global EV penetration rises from ~20% to ~40% over the next 3 years.
Competition among automakers is fierce and margins are being eaten by price
wars, but demand for the parts every EV must consume — power semiconductors
(SiC/IGBT) and charging infrastructure — grows rigidly regardless.

## Pass-through math (illustrative)

EV unit sales ×2 × rising power-semi content per vehicle (400V→800V platform
transition) ≈ power semiconductor demand ×2.5, against a 3–4 year capacity
expansion cycle → a temporary supply/demand gap.

## Payload assumption

EV penetration growth does not stall due to subsidy phase-outs or rising
electricity prices. If penetration growth stalls, the capacity expansion
underway turns directly into overcapacity.

## Beneficiary layers (by pass-through speed)

charging (months, utilization) → power-semis (quarters, orders) →
materials (years, long-term contracts)

## Kill conditions

- EV unit sales turn YoY-negative in any major market
- SiC capacity utilization falls below 80% (visible in leading suppliers' filings)
- 800V platform adoption falls short of expectations
